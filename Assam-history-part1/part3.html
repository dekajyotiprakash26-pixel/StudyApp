What is the Paik system?
The Paik system was a state-controlled labour and military organization in medieval Assam under the Ahom rule, where every adult male between 16 to 50 years was registered as a paik and had to render service to the State in rotation.
Topic: after pratap singha (1603)
Who was called a Paik?
A Paik was any adult male citizen of the Ahom kingdom who was enrolled for compulsory state service as a soldier in war and as a labourer in times of peace.
Topic: after pratap singha (1603)
What was the age limit for Paik registration?
Every male between the age of 16 and 50 years was compulsorily registered as a paik.
Topic: after pratap singha (1603)
What is a Got?
A Got was the smallest working unit under the Paik system, consisting of four paiks initially, later reduced to three paiks.
Topic: after pratap singha (1603)
How did the rotational service system in paik system work?
In each got, one paik served the State for three months a year (later four months), while the remaining paiks maintained his land and household.
Topic: after pratap singha (1603)
What was the main objective of the Paik system?
The main objective was to ensure a permanent supply of labour and military manpower to the State without paying regular salaries.
Topic: after pratap singha (1603)
What types of services did paiks render?
Paiks rendered military service during war and labour service such as agriculture, construction, and production during peace.
Topic: after pratap singha (1603)
What is a Khel?
A Khel was a professional and administrative group of paiks organised for a specific type of service like military, boat-building, arrow-making, or agriculture.
Topic: after pratap singha (1603)
How is a Khel different from a Got?
A Got was a small rotational unit for service, while a Khel was a large professional group responsible for a specific function of the State.
Topic: after pratap singha (1603)
Give examples of professional Khels.
Boat-building khel, arrow-making khel, cavalry khel, elephant-rearing khel.
Topic: after pratap singha (1603)

What is meant by rigid control of the State?

Topic: after pratap singha (1603)
What is Ga-mati?

Topic: after pratap singha (1603)
